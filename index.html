Project Description

Project consisted of approximately 18,000 square foot library for the City of Dallas. Project requires LEED Platinum Certification. The building includes reading room, office space, auditorium, classrooms, and men’s and women’s restrooms.

Demand Controlled Ventilation

Demand control ventilation was implemented in the areas of varying occupancy such as the reading area, and the meeting rooms. This system measures CO2 levels in the rooms and modulates the amount of outside air as needed to maintain acceptable levels of CO2 in the room.

Energy analysis

Luis Robles,under the supervision of Victor Meza, performed the energy model calculation with Trace 700. Mr. Robles completed and submitted the required LEED documentation for the MEP scope of work. The library achieved 10 points under Credit EA1 – Optimize Energy Performance for LEED NC version 3.0.

Geothermal Design

The energy model results were utilized to provide data for the GLD (Ground Loop Design) software to calculate number of wells and depth. In total 54 wells with a depth of 250 ft. and enhanced grout with a thermal conductivity of 0.85 (BTU/Hr * Ft * deg. F) were required for this project.

Greywater System

The building uses non-potable water for sewage conveyance, in addition to high-efficiency flush fixtures. 4.6 KGal of greywater is recycled and 8.34 KGal of HVAC condensate are recaptured and reused. 12.98 KGal is the total annual volume of nonpotable water reused for sewage conveyance, equivalent to a 87% reduction of potable water use for sewage conveyance.

Mechanical Systems

HVAC system utilizes geothermal heat pumps. Three air handlers serve the reading room area with an underfloor air distribution system. Individual heat pumps serve the Office Area, Classrooms, and Meeting Room. Each heat pump has an individual water circulation pump and can operate independently from other heat pumps.

Summary

While working for Meza Engineering, Luis Robles, assisted Victor Meza engineer the MEP systems for Prairie Creek Branch Library. Mr. Robles' responsibilities included; creating BIM model of the mechanical systems; generating equipment schedules, details, and riser diagrams; performing the energy analysis; the creation and submission of LEED documentation for the MEP systems.

Prairie Creek Branch Library was one of the first buildings to achieve LEED Platinum certification in Dallas, Texas in 2012. The building cooling and heating needs are provided by the geothermal/heat pump system. A daylight and artificial light system integrate for minimal energy consumption. A photovoltaic array is utilized to generate renewable energy. Water-efficient plumbing fixtures reduce the burden on municipal water supply and wastewater systems. Greywater and HVAC condensate are recaptured and reused.
